# Faceless Marketing Plan — Companion App

Owner: Gayathri (faceless — Blob is the face of the brand, never her).
Claude drafts everything (scripts, captions, posts, pages); she records short
screen clips and posts/schedules. Target: ~3-4 hrs/week of her time.

## Positioning
- Category nostalgia hook: "desktop pets are back" (Tamagotchi/Bonzi/Shimeji
  nostalgia) — but THIS one actually takes care of YOU.
- Emotional wedge vs Finch etc.: "an app that never guilts you. No streaks.
  No death. It just keeps you company."
- One-liner drafts: "A tiny creature lives on your desktop and cares about
  you." / "He juggles your to-dos. Literally."

## Content pillars (all screen-recorded, no face, no voice needed)
1. **Blob is alive** — 5-15s clips: drag him (he squeaks!), the juggle menu,
   him falling asleep at your bedtime, blinking idle. Trending audio +
   captions. Highest viral ceiling, zero production cost.
2. **Emotional moments** — "my desktop pet noticed I hadn't taken a break in
   3 hours" / bedtime care / sprout blooming when tasks get done. Relatable
   productivity-loneliness angle (WFH, students, ADHD-friendly framing —
   NEVER medical claims).
3. **Build-in-public (faceless)** — the AI-character pipeline story is itself
   content: "I can't draw or code. My AI turned this prompt into my app's
   character." Screen recordings + text. Great for X/Twitter + YouTube.
4. **Cozy desk-setup aesthetic** — Blob as part of aesthetic desktop tours
   (Pinterest/Instagram niche she already browses).

## Channels (ranked)
1. **TikTok + Instagram Reels + YouTube Shorts** — same vertical clip posted
   to all three (repurpose 1 recording = 3 posts). Desktop-pet content has a
   proven nostalgia audience. 3-4 clips/week.
2. **X/Twitter build-in-public** — text + clips; indie-app audience buys
   $5-7/mo apps. 3-5 posts/week (Claude drafts batches).
3. **Pinterest** — cozy desk aesthetic pins linking to the site; long shelf
   life, zero interaction needed. Batch 10 pins/month.
4. **Reddit** — r/macapps, r/SideProject, r/InternetIsBeautiful (launch
   moments only, authentic first-person posts, never spam).
5. **Product Hunt** — at public v1 (not beta). Assets prepared in advance.
6. **Micro-influencer seeding** — free lifetime codes to 20-30 cozy-produc-
   tivity / desk-setup creators (10k-100k followers). Faceless = they make
   the content for us.

## Funnel
Clip → link in bio → landing page (blob live demo GIF, one-liner, email
waitlist pre-launch → download+trial post-launch) → 7-day trial → $6.99/mo.
Waitlist NOW (before beta): every viral moment is wasted without somewhere
to send people.

## Timeline
- **Now → beta**: landing page + waitlist live; 2 clips/week (build-in-
  public + Blob-is-alive); X account "made by a tiny team & a tree creature".
- **Beta (friends/family)**: collect testimonials + reaction screenshots
  (with permission); keep posting; aim 500-1,000 waitlist emails.
- **Launch month**: Product Hunt + Reddit posts + influencer codes go out +
  daily clips for 2 weeks; waitlist email sequence (Claude writes).

## Claude produces / Gayathri does
- Claude: scripts+hooks, captions/hashtags, landing page (code+copy), email
  sequences, PH assets, pin descriptions, influencer outreach DMs, monthly
  calendar, analytics review.
- Gayathri: records clips (QuickTime, 15s), posts or schedules (Buffer/
  Later free tier), approves everything, replies to comments as "Blob's
  team" (or Blob himself — cuter).

## First 5 clip ideas (hooks)
1. "POV: you drag your desktop pet and he panics" (chipmunk squeak, dangle).
2. "My to-do app juggles." (menu open, slow-mo, trending audio).
3. "It's 11pm. My desktop pet just… went to sleep. Should I?" (bedtime).
4. "I told my desktop pet to remind me to call amma at 6." (voice → task).
5. "I can't draw. So an AI grew my app's mascot from one image." (pipeline
   before/after — build-in-public).

## Rules
- Never fake testimonials/reviews; never medical/mental-health claims;
  "ADHD-friendly" framing only as community language, not diagnosis.
- Privacy story is a FEATURE: "your chats never leave your device" is a
  marketing line, use it.
- Blob's voice in captions = the app's personality (warm, silly, no guilt).
